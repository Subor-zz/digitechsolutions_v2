## Production sequence

### Before low-fidelity UX sign-off

Subor approves audience priority; identifies two candidate cases and classifications; creates the initial claim ledger; supplies provisional scan inputs/scope/duration or marks the precise placeholder; confirms legal/contact owners. UX may proceed with content architecture while commercial price remains open, but proof sections cannot be treated as final.

### Before visual design

Approved homepage/service draft copy; case narratives or explicit lab fallback; scan report anatomy; founder bio; photography commissioning; diagram source facts; font and logo exploration; claim evidence review.

### Before launch

Final permissions/redactions, legal copy tied to chosen vendors, production images/social assets, form messages, SEO metadata, redirects based on performance data, accessibility text alternatives, and published-claim audit.

## Definition of content-ready

Every artifact has an owner, approval state, source and latest-review date. Every claim is evidenced or explicitly framed as an illustrative/lab assumption. No client data enters public assets without documented permission. Copy remains persuasive without motion and uses Digitech/`ik` consistently.
