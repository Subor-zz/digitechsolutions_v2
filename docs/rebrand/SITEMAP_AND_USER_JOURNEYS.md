## Navigation model

Desktop: logo; Expertise disclosure; Werkwijze; Cases; Insights; Over Subor; primary scan CTA. Mobile: same information order in a keyboard-safe disclosure, with Contact and legal links lower. Breadcrumbs on service, case and insight details. Each page has one visually dominant next step.

## User journeys

### 1. Operations-led

Search/referral → homepage recognition of handoffs → Hidden Drag → workflow route → workflow fit, exceptions and human controls → workflow-labelled case → scan workflow intake. Exit reassurance: they need not specify a tool or solution.

### 2. IT-led

Application/legacy insight or homepage → technical credibility immediately after business pain → application route → layers, options, migration and quality gates → evidence-labelled case/method → application scan. No language blames IT or guarantees rebuild.

### 3. Founder/product-led

Insight on technical debt/refactor → application modernization → phased choices and product constraints → About Subor/case → scan. Emphasize maintainability, ownership and bounded founder-led delivery rather than fractional-CTO breadth.

### 4. Returning credibility validator

Direct visit → Cases → inspect provenance, exclusions and result evidence → Method → About/verified credentials → scan/contact. Ensure critical proof is linkable, printable and understandable without motion.

### 5. Unsure: process or application

Homepage/scan → combined recognition statement → simple triage questions → combined route explanation → common output and explicit scope boundary → scan intake with `onzeker/gecombineerd`. Never force visitors to diagnose architecture themselves.

## Content linking rules

Every service links to at least one relevant case and insight when real content exists; details link back to their service and scan route. Never show empty proof carousels or fabricate related items. If launch proof is insufficient, keep the case index small and explicit rather than adding concept content without labels.
