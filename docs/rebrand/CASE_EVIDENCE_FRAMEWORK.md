# Case Evidence Framework

## Purpose

Cases prove judgment, scope control and outcomes without overstating provenance. Classification appears before the summary on overview cards and detail pages. Evidence quality is communicated in words, not color alone.

## Classification rules

| Type | Allowed claims | Required disclosure | Evidence threshold | Screenshots/results/permission | Must not imply |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Client** | Verified work performed, decisions and measured results within approved context | Client name/sector and relationship only as permitted; dates/scope/limitations | Source artifacts plus client approval for public narrative and every attributable metric | Real, redacted screenshots; baseline/method/timeframe for results; written name/logo/quote/media permission | Broader scope, causation not established, ongoing endorsement or work performed solely by Digitech if shared |
| **Anonymized** | Verified work and bounded results with identifying details removed/generalized | `Geanonimiseerde klantcase`; what categories were changed and why; result context retained | Internal source pack, approver and re-identification risk review | Redacted/recreated factual visuals; metrics generalized only if still truthful; client permission or documented contractual/legal basis | Fiction, public client identity, exact sector/scale if identifying, logo/testimonial, unverifiable precision |
| **Lab** | What was intentionally built/tested, observed technical behavior and reproducible benchmark conditions | `Labcase — geen klantopdracht`; objective, constraints, environment and artificial data | Repository/version, test protocol, artifacts and reproducibility notes | Own screenshots and synthetic data; measurements include device/build/sample; no client permission needed | Client adoption, business savings, production reliability, real-user outcome or external endorsement |
| **Concept** | Proposed approach, hypothesis, design rationale and questions to validate | `Concept — niet uitgevoerd`; assumptions and untested elements | Decision rationale and cited sources; no outcome threshold because no execution | Clearly illustrative frames; no result metrics except labelled estimates/ranges from cited models | Delivery, validation, production use, measured result, testimonial or client relationship |

## Evidence hierarchy

1. **Direct artifact:** approved source screenshot, log aggregate, test report, decision record, workflow map or deployment record.
2. **Corroborated record:** client-approved report, acceptance sign-off or dated stakeholder confirmation.
3. **Documented observation:** dated notes with method, observer and limitation.
4. **Inference:** explicitly labelled interpretation based on named evidence.
5. **Assumption/hypothesis:** never stated as result; used only in analysis/concept sections.

Every key statement is tagged internally with evidence level and source ID. Public pages do not need badge clutter, but editorial review must trace the statement.

## Measurable result standard

A publishable metric records: definition, baseline, comparison state, measurement window, sample/population, collection method, material confounders, responsible reviewer and permission. Prefer `observed during [window]` over permanent causal language. If causation cannot be isolated, state contribution/association, not `caused` or `saved`.

Qualitative results require source and scope too: who observed it, when, in which workflow, and whether it is a quote, synthesis or team acceptance. Never convert opinion into a percentage.

## Screenshot and artifact rules

- Remove names, emails, IDs, customer records, credentials, tokens, URLs, tenant data and proprietary identifiers.
- Preserve an original in a controlled location only if authorized; publish an approved derivative with source ID and review date.
- Recreated visuals say `Gereconstrueerd op basis van projectdocumentatie`.
- Synthetic data says `Illustratieve data`; it cannot substantiate client results.
- Alt text/caption explains the evidentiary point. Decorative crops cannot stand in for proof.
- Record owner, licence and written permission for logos, quotes and third-party interfaces.

## Permission workflow

1. Case owner compiles source pack and proposed public statements.
2. Privacy/security reviewer redacts and assesses re-identification/confidentiality.
3. Claim owner verifies metrics and scope.
4. Client/rights holder approves exact name, logo, quote, screenshots and result wording where required.
5. Subor approves publication and classification.
6. Register review/expiry date; withdraw or update when permission/facts change.

## Acceptance gate

A case cannot enter high-fidelity design as “proof-ready” unless classification, disclosure, sources, permissions, result method, limitations and intentionally-not-built section are complete. An incomplete case may inform wireframe proportions only and must carry an internal `NOT APPROVED FOR PUBLICATION` marker.
