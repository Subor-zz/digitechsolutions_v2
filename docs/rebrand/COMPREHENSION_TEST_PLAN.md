# Comprehension Test Plan

## Research objective

Determine whether the low-fidelity structure communicates what Digitech does, for whom, how workflow differs from application modernization, why the scan is the first step, and what founder-led/AI-supported delivery means—without visual polish or facilitator explanation.

This is a qualitative diagnostic with five participants, not statistical validation. Use the same clickable low-fidelity prototype and neutral script. Test desktop or mobile according to each participant’s normal behavior, with at least two mobile sessions.

## Participant matrix

| Participant | Required profile | Primary journey |
|---|---|---|
| P1 Operations decision-maker | COO/operations/bedrijfsvoering in ~20–250-person Dutch SME/scale-up | A |
| P2 IT decision-maker | IT/application manager, product owner or technical lead evaluating legacy/integration risk | B |
| P3 Founder/product owner | Owns an MVP/internal tool that has outgrown its current implementation | C |
| P4 Less-technical business owner | Feels operational drag but cannot name technical cause | E |
| P5 Neutral professional | Unfamiliar with Digitech; capable of evaluating business websites but not in target role | D/control |

Exclude close collaborators who already know the positioning. Record recruitment fit and prior familiarity. Do not show the strategic brief.

## Session structure (35–45 minutes)

1. Consent, recording permission and context: 3 minutes.
2. Five-second homepage exposure: 3 minutes.
3. Thirty-second self-directed scan: 4 minutes.
4. Assigned journey task: 12–15 minutes.
5. Scan route/intake task: 7 minutes.
6. Proof/founder/AI questions: 7 minutes.
7. Debrief and confidence rating: 4 minutes.

Facilitator says: `We testen het ontwerp, niet jou. Denk hardop. Ik kan vragen wat je verwacht, maar leg de site niet uit.`

## Tasks

### Task 1 — Five-second meaning

Show the homepage top for five seconds, hide it, then ask: `Wat doet deze organisatie, voor wie en wat zou je hier als volgende stap kunnen doen?`

Capture unaided wording before probes. Do not accept a later corrected answer as 5-second success.

### Task 2 — Choose a route

Give a role-matched scenario:

- P1: requests pass through inbox, Excel and manual checks.
- P2: an internal application is hard to update and has fragile integrations.
- P3: an MVP works but lacks tests/documentation and is difficult to scale.
- P4: work is slow but cause is unclear.
- P5: referral says Digitech may help; validate fit and credibility.

Ask the participant to find the most relevant path and explain why.

### Task 3 — Understand the scan

Ask them to determine what the Modernization Scan does, what they expect to receive, whether it assumes a build and what information they must supply. Then start the intake and select a route.

### Task 4 — Validate credibility

Ask: `Voordat je contact opneemt, controleer of je deze partij geloofwaardig vindt.` Observe use of case classification, evidence, limitations, Method and About. Ask what additional proof they need.

### Task 5 — Recover

Present one validation error and one simulated submission failure. Ask the participant to recover or choose another contact route. At least one session uses reduced-motion preference and all mobile sessions use the stacked diagram states.

## Interview questions

Ask after behavior, not before:

- In one sentence, what does Digitech Solutions do?
- Who is the primary customer?
- What is the difference between workflow and application modernization?
- What would you choose if you were unsure? Could you choose incorrectly?
- What is the Modernization Scan: sales call, audit, project or something else?
- What do you expect to receive, and does it have value without follow-on work?
- What role does AI play? What does it not do?
- Who would perform the work? How large do you think Digitech is?
- Which claim or proof did you trust most/least, and why?
- What does founder-led add or subtract for you?
- Which term or sentence was unclear or sounded like marketing language?
- What would stop you from starting the intake?
- What sensitive information, if any, did you think the form wanted?

Rate 1–5: clarity, relevance, technical confidence, trust, scan attractiveness and confidence in next step. Ratings explain direction; quotations/behavior explain why.

## Success criteria

| Measure | Pass threshold |
|---|---|
| What Digitech does | 4/5 mention improving workflows/processes and/or modernizing applications; across sample both routes are represented |
| Audience | 4/5 identify organizations/operations/IT rather than consumers or generic startups only |
| Route distinction | 4/5 correctly distinguish process/handoffs from software/legacy and recognize overlap |
| Scan | 4/5 describe diagnosis/roadmap before building and standalone outputs; none call it only a free sales call |
| CTA | 5/5 identify the scan as primary next step without prompting |
| AI | 4/5 understand AI as supported delivery under human controls, not the customer-facing service |
| Company size | 5/5 understand founder-led/primarily Subor; none infer a large internal delivery team |
| Proof | 4/5 notice classification/limitations; no participant mistakes lab/concept for client work |
| Journey | 4/5 reach relevant scan route without facilitator rescue; uncertain participant selects unsure/combined |
| Form safety | 5/5 understand not to submit credentials/code/sensitive data and can recover from errors |

High-fidelity design cannot use style to mask a failure in what Digitech does, route distinction, proof provenance or scan comprehension.

## Failure signals

- “AI agency”, “software developer for anything”, “temporary IT staffing” or “large agency” is the dominant interpretation.
- Operations participant feels blamed; IT participant sees no credible technical depth.
- `combined` is perceived as premium/enterprise rather than intertwined diagnosis.
- Scan is mistaken for free consultation, full audit, fixed-price build or mandatory presales.
- Participants seek price/duration so strongly that the page feels incomplete.
- Founder-led reads as lack of capacity without adequate scope/partner explanation.
- Case classification is missed or proof cards imply unsupported client success.
- Form route feels irreversible, technical fields cause guessing, errors lose data, or sensitive material seems requested.
- Motion/static diagrams change the information available.

## Note-taking format

| Time/frame | Task | Observed action/path | Verbatim quote | Interpretation | Severity | Theme | Follow-up |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
|  |  |  |  |  | 0–3 | message/nav/proof/scan/form/founder/AI |  |

Separate observation from interpretation. Severity: 0 preference, 1 friction, 2 task failure with recovery, 3 critical misunderstanding/no recovery. Record first-click, route chosen, time-to-route, facilitator help and final confidence.

## Synthesis method

1. Each observer independently tags notes before discussion.
2. Build an affinity map by message, navigation, proof, scan, form, founder and AI.
3. Create an issue matrix: frequency × severity × strategic risk.
4. Prioritize any single P2/P3 misunderstanding involving positioning, false proof, privacy or route failure even if only one participant encounters it.
5. Map each issue to exact frame/copy and owner; label `fix before visual`, `test variant`, `content dependency` or `later`.
6. Update decisions only for findings supported by behavior and locked-strategy compatibility.
7. Retest changed 5-second proposition and scan route with at least two relevant participants if a critical issue changes structure.

## Research deliverables

Recruitment screener, consent/script, prototype link/version, raw notes, issue matrix, journey completion table, 5/30-second comprehension summary, evidence requests, recommended changes and decision-log update. Store recordings/participant PII outside the public repository with access and deletion dates.
