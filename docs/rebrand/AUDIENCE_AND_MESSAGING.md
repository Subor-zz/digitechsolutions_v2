# Audience and Messaging

## Audience priority decision

Lead with **operational friction**, then establish technical credibility in the same viewport/story beat. This prioritizes the person who feels delay and error while giving IT enough evidence that Digitech will not automate around architecture, security or ownership. Workflow and application modernization are two diagnosis-led routes, not two unrelated businesses.

### Buyers

| Priority | Buyer | Job to be done | Needed signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary | Operations/bedrijfsvoering leader in a Dutch SME or scale-up | Reduce handoffs, rework, waiting and dependence without destabilizing work | Business recognition, bounded first step, adoption and control |
| Secondary | IT/application owner | Reduce legacy, integration and continuity risk while preserving service | Technical method, evidence, phased change and transferability |
| Tertiary | Founder/product owner | Make an MVP/internal tool maintainable and scalable | Product judgment, explicit trade-offs and delivery credibility |

The economic buyer may be operations or the founder; IT is often a required evaluator and delivery stakeholder. Copy must never frame IT as the obstacle.

## Pains, outcomes and objections

| Pains | Desired outcomes | Likely objection | Response/proof needed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Email/spreadsheet handoffs, duplicate entry, exceptions found late | Shorter, visible, controlled flow | “You do not understand our exceptions.” | Current-state map, exception paths, human gates |
| Legacy code, unsupported dependencies, fragile integrations | Safer staged modernization | “This becomes an unnecessary rewrite.” | Decision lens, exclusions, migration/rollback logic |
| Knowledge concentrated in one person/vendor | Documented ownership and handover | “A solo specialist creates new dependence.” | Artifacts, version control, documentation and transparent specialists |
| Unclear whether process or software is the root issue | Evidence-based route and priorities | “A scan is paid presales.” | Standalone deliverables and usable roadmap |
| Limited internal delivery capacity | Bounded first step and credible execution route | “Can one founder deliver this?” | Scope limits, direct accountability, partner disclosure |
| AI hype/security concern | Faster delivery with review and control | “AI means black-box or careless code.” | Human review, testing, security and no sensitive-data claims |

## Trust requirements

Before conversion, visitors need: a precise problem definition; clear fit/non-fit; a tangible scan output; visible founder identity; evidence-labelled cases; a controlled delivery method; privacy-safe intake; technical limits; no automatic rewrite promise; and a credible next step even if Digitech does not execute.

## First 30 seconds

1. **Recognition (0–5 s):** “Moderniseer wat je organisatie vertraagt.” Support with workflows and applications, not job titles.
2. **Mechanism (5–12 s):** hidden drag lives in handoffs, data and aging software; Digitech maps it before choosing a solution.
3. **Technical confidence (12–20 s):** process, data, systems, risk and ownership; simplify/integrate/refactor/rebuild are options, not assumptions.
4. **Choice (20–25 s):** workflow route, application route, or “I’m not sure”.
5. **Safe action (25–30 s):** Modernization Scan produces a current-state map, prioritized risks/options and phased roadmap.

## Homepage proposition

**Headline:** Moderniseer wat je organisatie vertraagt.

**Support:** Digitech Solutions brengt vastgelopen workflows en verouderde bedrijfsapplicaties terug naar een beheersbare route—eerst door proces, techniek en risico scherp te krijgen, daarna met gerichte uitvoering en overdracht.

**Credibility line:** Rechtstreeks met Subor Cheung. AI-ondersteund waar het versnelt; menselijk beoordeeld, getest en gedocumenteerd.

The headline remains a working line pending comprehension testing; it is strong enough for wireframes.

## Supporting message hierarchy

1. Friction is often between systems and teams.
2. Diagnose before automation, refactor or rebuild.
3. One story, two expertise routes: workflow and application.
4. Modernize in controlled, transferable steps.
5. Founder-led accountability with transparent specialist input.
6. The scan is useful even without a follow-on build.

## CTA hierarchy

- **Primary sitewide:** `Start met een Modernization Scan` → scan page, never directly to an opaque scheduler.
- **Secondary contextual:** `Bekijk hoe de scan werkt`, `Bekijk de werkwijze`, `Bekijk [route]`.
- **Proof CTA:** `Bekijk de case` only when evidence/classification is publishable.
- **Fallback:** `Plan een verkennend gesprek` or email on scan/contact pages; present as orientation, not a free audit.

Avoid “Beschikbaarheid bespreken”, hourly-rate-first language and multiple equal hero buttons. Visitors who are unsure select “Workflow en applicatie zitten samen vast / ik weet het nog niet.”

## Copy guardrails

Use concrete nouns (overdracht, wachttijd, dependency, module, eigenaar), state exclusions, and distinguish observation from assumption. Use `Digitech Solutions` for the business and `ik/Subor` for personal delivery; reserve `we` for an explicitly named joint client team. Never imply a multi-person internal agency, guaranteed savings, instant availability or universal stack expertise.

## Validation in low-fidelity UX

Test with at least two operations and two IT evaluators plus one founder. After 5 and 30 seconds ask: what does Digitech solve, which first step is offered, is a rewrite assumed, and who delivers? Pass when 4/5 identify both modernization routes, the scan and founder-led controlled approach without prompting.
