**Date:** 18 July 2026
**Verdict:** the low-fidelity **specification** is complete. Actual clickable wireframes and user testing are the next sprint. Full high-fidelity visual design is not yet authorized.

- Page goals, audiences, intent, hierarchy, section order, CTAs, density, trust/proof, mobile/accessibility, SEO, dependencies and exits for 15 launch page/state types.

## Can high-fidelity visual design begin?

**Not as a full-site design sprint.** It would force visual decisions around untested hierarchy and empty/unverified proof. The next gate requires:

1. representative desktop and 320 px wireframes completed;
2. five-person comprehension test run and critical findings resolved;
3. minimum scan facts approved;
4. two case candidates classified with credible evidence plans;
5. founder/claim inputs sufficient to size real content.

After those conditions, high-fidelity may begin with the visual-foundation spike: moodboard, font/contrast tests, three logo directions and static hero/Hidden Drag keyframes. Final case, scan and About screens still require publication-approved content.

## Does repository recovery block the next sprint?

**No. The canonical repository and application source are confirmed and assessed.** Figma wireframes, content/evidence work and user research can proceed. Production HTML/components, form integration, redirects and deployment work remain gated by UX/visual approval, rebuild boundaries, provider decisions and Vercel role/rollback verification—not by missing source.

### Exit criteria

- 4/5 accurately explain the offer, audience, route distinction and scan; 5/5 identify scan CTA and founder-led size.
- Unsure participant reaches `unsure/combined` without assistance.
- No lab/concept is mistaken for client proof and no sensitive data appears requested.
- All five journeys complete at 320 px with specified keyboard/focus behavior.
- Critical comprehension/privacy/evidence failures are fixed and retested where necessary.
- Subor has supplied minimum scan facts, case candidates and founder evidence.
